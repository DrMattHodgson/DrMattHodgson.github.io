Conferences

Psi-k

Psi-k, originally planned for 2020, was postponed due to the Covid-19 pandemic. It took place in Lausanne, Switzerland from 22nd-25th August 2022. The conference hosted 7 plenary talks and 42 symposia accommodating 130 invited talks and open slots for over 180 contributed talks. I found the conference to be an excellent opportunity for networking.

Modern Techniques in Theory and Calculation

I was invited by Nankai University, China to present at their online symposium Modern Techniques in Theory and Calculation on the 10th Jun 2022. My talk Should discontinuities in the Kohn-Sham potential be avoided or exploited? (slides) discusses the origin of the infamous discontinuous features of the exact Kohn-Sham potential and what modern techniques can be used to capture this non-analytic behaviour.

Collaborative Computational Project for the Study of the Electronic Structure of Condensed Matter

CCP9 connects several UK research groups and facilitates UK participation in other European networks. I've attended the conference twice in York: once in 2015 (poster) and again in 2016 (prize winning poster), and in Manchester in 2022 (talk).

Royal Society of Chemistry's

New horizons in density functional theory Faraday Discussion

The 2020 Faraday Discussion New horizons in density functional theory was held online from 2nd-4th Sep owing to the COVID-19 pandemic. Dr Nikitas Gidopoulos presented our contribution Improving the exchange and correlation potential in density-functional approximations through constraints.

Our general discussion titled New approaches to study excited states in density functional theory was also published.

European Theoretical Spectroscopy Facility's

Young Researchers' Meeting

The European Theoretical Spectroscopy Facility (ETSF) Young Researchers' Meeting (YRM) is held annually for scientists with non-permanent positions — it is unusual in this sense. The first year I attended as a PhD student in Paris, France, in 2015 (slides). The following year the conference was held in London, UK (poster). In 2017 I gave a talk in Tarragona, Spain (slides). In 2018 the meeting was held in Hamburg, Germany (slides − credit Dr. Jack Wetherell). In 2019 the conference was held in San Sebastián, Spain (slides).

Notre Dame, Paris, France, 2015

Correlation Team's Meeting

The ETSF Correlation Team's meeting is usually held every 6 months at the École Polytechnique in Palaiseau, France. The meeting is a unique chance to discuss new ideas concerning the development of first-principles methods for modelling electron correlation (a hugely challenging aspect of many-electron theory) in a friendly and constructive environment. Dr. Jack Wetherell and I attended the meeting from 18th - 19th of October, 2018 and again from the 8th - 9th of October, 2019. We jointly presented our work on many-body perturbation theory and generalised Kohn-Sham theory to a receptive audience who gave valuable feedback.

The Hebrew University of Jerusalem

Exact Factorisation for Electrons, Nuclei and Photons

In 2019 I gave a talk at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem (slides). The workshop was focused on the exact factorisation theory which offers a way to go beyond the Born-Oppenheimer approximation within electronic structure calculation. I was invited along with other speakers to attend the workshop and present my work with the aim of developing collaborations among the community.

The Hebrew University of Jerusalem, Jerusalem, Israel, 2019

American Physical Society's

March Meeting

The APS March Meeting hosts approximately 10,000 physicists each year. With over 100 invited speakers and nearly 550 contributed sessions, it is the largest condensed matter physics conference in the world. I attended the conference in San Antonio, Texas in 2015 (abstract and slides) and Los Angles, California in 2018 (abstract and slides) — both times I presented a talk on my research.

Dr Eli Kraisler (abstract) and Dr Jack Wetherell (abstract) also presented our work at the 2018 March Meeting.

Los Angeles Convention Center, Los Angeles, California, 2018

Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft's

Spring Meeting

The Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft (DPG) holds a Spring Meeting every year which is one of the largest conferences for physics in Europe. In 2017 the conference was held in Dresden and in 2018 it was held in Berlin; I attended these conferences and gave a double talk in Berlin with my colleague Dr. Eli Kraisler (slides).
