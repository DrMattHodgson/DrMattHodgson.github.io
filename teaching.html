Teaching

Advanced Theoretical Techniques

During my Advanced Theoretical Techniques module we study the mathematical tools which underpin modern theoretical physics. With these powerful techniques, my students are able to solve advanced problems in theoretical physics and beyond. The course consists of 18 lectures in which we study

  • Integral transforms, such as Fourier and Laplace
  • The calculus of variations, e.g. Lagrangians for fields
  • The transformation of physical laws between reference frames, e.g. those employed in Special and General Relativity.

All my lecture notes are available to students via the virtual learning environment (VLE).

Computational & Mathematical Techniques for Theoretical Physics

In the Computational & Mathematical Techniques for Theoretical Physics module my students learn to solve mathematical problems analytically (for example within complex analysis) and, for more complicated sets of equations, numerically. Most research in physics today relies on theoretical and computational simulation of nature. My course offers an introduction to describing real-world phenomena with mathematical and computational models.

During my lectures I use Jupyter notebooks to present computer code in an accessible way. Students can access all my notebooks and lecture notes via the VLE.

Advanced Computational Lab

I am head of the third-year Advanced Computational Lab in the first term. During my lab, students use the skills they have developed in computational and theoretical physics over their 3 years of study to tackle challenging, research-style physics problems.

Second-year Mathematics

I teach second-year mathematics, which spans over two terms (36 lectures). This module is core to the students' learning and covers some of the most important areas of mathematical physics, for example

  • Vector calculus
  • Linear algebra
  • Solving second-order differential equations
  • Fourier series and transform
  • Lagrangian and Hamiltonian mechanics.
All lecture notes are accessible through the VLE.
